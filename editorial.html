The Struggle Against
Jeepney Modernization

However, a cloud of uncertainly looms as the government pushes forward with the jeepney Modernization Program (PUVMP), aiming to replace the beloved traditional jeepneys with modern counter parts. Transport group led be Pagkakaisa ng mga Samahan ng tsuper at Operator Nationwide (piston), have vehemently opposed this initiative, citing concerns about its impact on both operators and commuters.


One voice echoing the sentiments of
many jeepney drivers is that of Mr. Raquelino Dimatawaran, a seasoned driver with over two decades of experience. His words encapsulate the sentiments shared by a significant portion of the transport sector, expressing a deep sense of sadness at the prospect of losing the traditional jeepney that has been an integral part of his identity since childhood.

As the government defends the PUVMP, Transport Secretary Ferdinand Marcos Jr. attempts to persuade transport groups to reconsider their stance, emphasizing the


inevitability of modernization While the acknowledging the lack of preparedness in implementing the program, Marcos Jr. assures a different way" forward, promising a thorough review and underscoring the program's significance in revitalizing the Philippine economy

The concems voiced by Mr. Dimatawaran and other transport groups have prompted legislative action, with the Senate deliberating on Senate Bill No. 105. This proposed legislation aims to provide a "Just and humane" PUV modernization program, offering a minimum 20% subsidy for operators opting to modernize, a loan program for vehicle acquisition, and a compensation program for operators unable to continue in the PUV sector due to the program.

Friday Catch-Up Day:
New Chapter In Education Unfold

In a groundbreaking move, the Department of Education (DepEd) has unveiled a transformative initiative slated to commence on January 12, 2024. Vice President and Education Secretary Sara Duterte revealed that every Friday will now be designated as a "catch-up" day in schools nationwide.

During these dedicated Fridays, students from kindergarten to Grade 12 will immerse themselves in the world of literature, fostering a culture of reading and comprehension. Vice President Duterte emphasized that this time will be utilized for delving into books, articles, and various reading materials aligned with Individual Interests.

The Friday Catch-Up Day marks a departure from traditional educational norms, embracing a holistic perspective on learning. Vice President Duterte envisions this initiative as a catalyst for intellectual growth, empowering students to become avid readers. analytical thinkers, and proficient writers
